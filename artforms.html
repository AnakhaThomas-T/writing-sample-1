Exploring the Colors, Music and Divinity of Kerala

Kathakali

Kathakali, an ancient classical Indian dance form, remains a vibrant theatre tradition, particularly practiced by Malayali people in the southwestern state of Kerala. Recognized for its intricate make-up and costumes, this "story game" genre showcases the artistry of male actor-dancers.

Mohiniyattam

Mohiniyattam is a classical dance form that originated in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is one of the eight major Indian classical dance forms and is known for its graceful and feminine movements. The name “Mohiniyattam” is derived from the words “Mohini,” meaning a female avatar of the Hindu god Vishnu, and “attam,” meaning dance.

Theyyam

Theyyam is a grand dance festival in Kerala and is celebrated many regions of the state including Kasargod. Theyyam is considered as Dance Gods and the name has been given to it by adding two words ‘Deivam’ and ‘Aattam’, where ‘Deivam’ means God and ‘Aattam’ translates to dance. This dance is performed to honour the heroes and ancestral spirits.
